Flower Shopping App

The Problem

Bhutanese flower buyers face challenges with unreliable deliveries, unclear product expectations, and inefficient ordering processes. Frequent buyers like Sagar struggle with slow checkout and lack of customization, while occasional buyers like Tsheten find it difficult to choose appropriate flowers for specific occasions. Current solutions rely on scattered social media pages and physical stores with inconsistent quality and delivery times.

Our Solution

A centralized flower shopping app with quick checkout for frequent buyers and guided navigation for occasional purchasers. Features include occasion-based categorization, real-time delivery tracking, saved preferences for fast reordering, and accurate product visualization. The app will offer reliable scheduling, secure payment confirmation, and personalized recommendations to address the needs of both user types identified in our research.

The Goal

Our flower shopping app will streamline the purchasing process for both frequent and occasional buyers in Bhutan. For users like Sagar, we aim to reduce checkout time by 50% through saved preferences and quick reordering. For users like Tsheten, we target a 40% improvement in purchase confidence through occasion-based recommendations and clear product information. Success will be measured by delivery reliability (target: 95% on-time deliveries), reduced customer service inquiries about orders, and increased repeat purchases.

Project Timeline
March - June 2024



User Research

Through interviews with frequent buyers like Sagar and occasional buyers like Tsheten, we identified key pain points in Bhutan's flower purchasing experience. Frequent buyers expressed frustration with unreliable deliveries and inefficient ordering processes, while occasional buyers struggled with choosing appropriate flowers and understanding delivery options. Our research revealed that 100% of frequent buyers wanted faster checkout and customization options, while 90% of occasional buyers preferred guided navigation by occasion. These findings directly informed our design priorities: quick reordering features for power users, occasion-based categorization for new users, and real-time delivery tracking for all customers.

Key Pain Points Identified

From our research with flower buyers, we discovered these critical challenges

Unreliable Deliveries

Late deliveries and lack of tracking cause anxiety, especially for time-sensitive occasions like birthdays and anniversaries.

Inefficient Ordering

Frequent buyers waste time re-entering information, while occasional buyers struggle with complex navigation.

Unclear Product Expectations

Received bouquets often differ from expectations due to poor product visualization and descriptions.

Poor Discovery

Occasional buyers can't easily find flowers suitable for specific events or recipients due to limited filtering.

Usability Study Finding

Usability testing was conducted with users to evaluate the flower shop’s digital services, including browsing flowers, placing orders, and tracking deliveries.

Unclear Order Confirmation: Users were unsure whether their flower orders or payments were successful due to vague or delayed confirmation messages.

Desire for Notification System: Participants wanted real-time notifications (via SMS or email) for order confirmations, delivery status, and payment receipts.

Accessibility Considerations

Form Labels and Instructions

All form fields for placing orders and delivery scheduling—such as flower type selection, date/time pickers, and recipient details—are clearly labeled with descriptive text and guidance to support users with cognitive impairments or those using screen readers.

Focus Indicators

Interactive elements like category filters, cart buttons, and checkout forms have visible focus states, ensuring that keyboard-only users can easily follow their navigation path throughout the site.

Consistent Navigation

A uniform and intuitive navigation structure is maintained across the platform, helping users seamlessly explore floral collections, place orders, and track deliveries—particularly aiding users with cognitive or visual difficulties.
