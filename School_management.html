School Management System

The Problem

Bhutanese university students and their parents struggle with inefficient school communication systems. Students like Sagar miss important academic updates and fee deadlines due to reliance on physical notice boards and verbal reminders, while boarding students like Dal face challenges communicating with parents about school matters. Parents lack real-time access to their children's academic progress and fee status.

Our Solution

Develop a comprehensive School Management System that provides real-time academic updates, fee tracking, and communication tools. The system will feature a student portal with calendar reminders, a parent portal for monitoring academic progress and payments, and digital fee payment options. This centralized platform will eliminate reliance on physical notices and improve school-home communication.

The Goal

Our School Management System will provide students and parents with timely academic updates, fee reminders, and direct communication channels. We will measure success through reduced missed deadlines, increased on-time fee payments, and higher parent engagement. The system aims to decrease stress for students like Sagar and improve information access for parents of boarding students like Dal.

Project Timeline
March - June 2024



User Research

Through interviews with RUB students like Sagar and Dal, we identified critical communication gaps in Bhutan's education system. Sagar, a day scholar, often misses important updates that are only posted on physical notice boards, while Dal, a boarding student, struggles to keep her parents informed about academic and financial matters. Parents reported difficulty tracking their children's progress between parent-teacher meetings. Our research revealed that 100% of interviewed students wanted a centralized digital platform for school updates, and 80% of parents expressed interest in a parent portal. These findings directly informed our design priorities: real-time updates, fee tracking, and bilingual accessibility (English/Dzongkha) to serve both tech-savvy and less digital-literate users.

Key Pain Points Identified

From our research with students and parents, we discovered these critical challenges

Inefficient Communication Channels

Reliance on physical notice boards and verbal reminders leads to missed updates about exams, fees, and school events.

Fee Payment Challenges

Students and parents struggle with unclear deadlines, late payments, and lack of payment confirmation systems.

Parent-School Disconnect

Parents of boarding students have limited visibility into their child's academic progress between parent-teacher meetings.

Language Barriers

Some parents prefer Dzongkha interfaces but most systems are English-only, creating accessibility issues.

Usability Study Finding

Usability research was conducted through interviews with students, parents, and school staff to evaluate the effectiveness of current academic communication and administrative workflows.

Communication Gaps: Students, especially boarders like Dal, struggled to inform their parents about exam schedules and fee payments due to limited or delayed communication methods.

Lack of Centralized Information: Participants, such as Sagar, frequently missed key updates (e.g., exam timetables, fee deadlines) because there was no single platform consolidating all academic information.

Desire for Real-Time Updates and Notifications: Both students and parents expressed a need for a centralized app with real-time academic alerts, automated fee reminders, and a parent portal to track progress and payments easily.

Accessibility Considerations

Form Labels and Instructions

All input fields—such as fee payment forms, exam registration, and profile updates—are clearly labeled with descriptive text and instructions to support users with cognitive impairments or those using assistive technologies like screen readers.

Focus Indicators

Interactive elements like buttons for checking schedules, paying fees, and accessing report cards include visible focus states, ensuring seamless navigation for keyboard-only users and improved accessibility.

Consistent Navigation

The platform maintains a consistent layout across dashboards for students, parents, and staff, helping users with cognitive difficulties stay oriented while accessing academic records, reminders, and communication tools.
